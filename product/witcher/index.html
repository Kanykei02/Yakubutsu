    Final version Created in 2002, the CD Projekt RED STUDIO division of the Polish company CD Projekt began developing The Witcher in 2004. The plot was developed by Polish fantasy writers Jacek Komuda and Maciej Jurevich. Andrzej Sapkowski also took part in the development of the game's plot. According to the original idea of ​​the authors of the game, the main character was not supposed to be the main character of the cycle of novels - Geralt, but an unknown monster hunter, but later the authors abandoned this idea.

    It is known that the developers used motion capture technology to depict Geralt's movements during battle scenes; in this they were assisted by one of the most famous experts in medieval weapons and fencing in Poland, Marcin Żmudzki (Polish: Marcin Żmudzki).The Polish post-production studio Platige Image was responsible for the creation of the game clips.

    In the version of the game released in the USA, the dialogues were heavily edited (mostly, the rude vocabulary was removed) and some pictures from the diary of the main character.On May 16, 2008 the improved edition of The Witcher: Enhanced Edition was released, which contains a modified version of the game with improved gameplay, interface and graphics, as well as additional materials.
